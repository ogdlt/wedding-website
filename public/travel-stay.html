02
Getting around

Transport within Valencia

The Friday welcome cocktail takes place at Parador El Saler. If you're staying at the Parador, there's no transfer needed — if you're at the second hotel, you'll be very close by and taxis are easy to arrange between the two.

On Saturday 10 July, we'll have buses from Parador El Saler to the ceremony and back. Guests staying at the second hotel can hop in a quick taxi to the Parador to catch the bus. We don't recommend renting a car for Friday or Saturday — the buses and taxis have you covered, so you can just relax and enjoy the weekend.

Friday Cocktail at Parador El Saler — walking distance or a short taxi from the second hotel
Saturday Buses from Parador El Saler to the venue and back (taxis easily available from the second hotel)
Tip Skip the rental car for Fri & Sat — just enjoy
03
Getting to Valencia

Flights & trains

We recommend flying into Valencia Airport (VLC), which has plenty of international connections. If you're travelling within Spain, high-speed trains run to Valencia Joaquín Sorolla station, just a short taxi from the city centre.

Airport Valencia Airport (VLC) — international connections
Train Valencia Joaquín Sorolla (AVE high-speed rail)
From airport ~20 min by taxi to El Saler
From Sorolla ~25 min by taxi to El Saler
